# Project Brief

## Identity

- Name: VaultFront
- Slug: vaultfront
- Type: browser-based real-time strategy game
- Status: in-development (runtime pre-launch, pages-stub live)
- Owner: VaultSparkStudios

## Why this exists

- **Problem:** Real-time strategy games in browsers are either too shallow (clicker
  mechanics) or too complex (hours of learning curve). OpenFrontIO proves the format
  has an audience; VaultFront proves it can have depth.
- **Opportunity:** Add a logistics layer — vault sites, convoy routing, risk scoring,
  comeback mechanics — that rewards strategic decision-making without requiring APM
  or prior RTS experience.
- **Audience:** Players who enjoyed browser strategy in the 2010s and want something
  that respects their intelligence without demanding their calendar.

## What makes VaultFront different from OpenFrontIO

1. **Vault sites** — contested map objectives that pay out gold + troops on delivery
2. **Convoy routing** — players choose where loot travels; riskier routes pay more
3. **Execution chain** — capture → deliver → jam is a 3-step combo that rewards
   coordination and pays +20% on the next convoy
4. **Comeback surge** — 6 minutes behind triggers a 2-minute gold + troop bonus
5. **Squad objectives** — coordinated vault plays reward both players
6. **Weekly mutators** — lane_fog, accelerated_cooldowns, double_passive rotate weekly
   to keep the meta fresh

## Success criteria

- **Shipped:** `play-vaultfront.vaultsparkstudios.com` is live and accepting real players
- **Polished:** HUD surfaces all six mechanics visibly (combo meter, surge badge, squad ring)
- **Retained:** Average session count per player ≥ 3 in the first 30 days post-launch

## Scope

- In scope: Core gameplay, replay system, spectator mode, map editor (after launch),
  weekly mutators, Discord community feed
- Out of scope: Mobile native app, paid cosmetics store, tournament bracket system
  (all post-v1.0)
